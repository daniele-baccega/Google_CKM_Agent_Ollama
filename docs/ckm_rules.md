# CKM Clinical Guidelines & Protocols

## SGLT2 Inhibitor Management (Cardiorenal Protection)
**Indication:** All HFrEF patients, Type 2 DM with CKD or HF (Class IA ESC 2023, AHA 2024)
- **Drugs:** Empagliflozin 10 mg daily OR Dapagliflozin 10 mg daily
- **Benefits:** Reduces HF hospitalization (27%), CKD progression (39%), cardiovascular death (18%)
- **Initiation:** Safe to start in CKD Stage 3a–3b (eGFR 30–59); monitor eGFR and Cr
- **Monitoring:** Check eGFR at baseline, 2 weeks, then monthly × 3, then q3mo
- **Peri-op:** Hold 3–4 days pre-op to reduce euglycemic DKA risk; resume post-op if no acute illness
- **Contraindications:** Type 1 DM (euglycemic DKA risk), stage 5 CKD (eGFR <15)

## MRA (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist) Protocol
**Indication:** All HFrEF with eGFR >30 and K+ <5.0 mEq/L (Class IA ESC 2023)
- **First-line:** Spironolactone 12.5–25 mg daily (titrate to 25 mg if tolerated)
- **Alternative:** Eplerenone 25–50 mg daily (if spironolactone intolerance)
- **Mortality benefit:** 30% reduction in all-cause death, 35% reduction in HF hospitalization
- **Monitoring:** K+, Cr at baseline, 3 days, 1 week, then monthly × 3, then q3mo
- **Hyperkalemia risk:** Mitigated by concurrent SGLT2 inhibitor (lowers K+ by ~0.3 mEq/L)
- **Peri-op:** Continue unless acute renal injury or K+ >5.5 mEq/L

## ACE-Inhibitor / ARB Uptitration (RAAS Optimization)
**Target Doses for HFrEF (ESC 2023, Class IA):**
- Lisinopril: 20–35 mg daily (usual max 40 mg)
- Enalapril: 10 mg BID (usual max 20 mg BID)
- Ramipril: 5 mg BID (usual max 10 mg BID)
- Losartan: 150 mg daily (Class IB for HFrEF)
- Valsartan: 320 mg daily (with sacubitril in ARNI)
- Sacubitril/Valsartan: 97/103 mg BID (most effective, Class IA)

**Titration Strategy in CKD Stage 3:**
- Start low, go slow; uptitrate by 50% every 2–4 weeks if tolerated
- Monitor: Cr and K+ at each step
- Stop or reduce if: SBP <90 mmHg (hold, reassess), eGFR drops >25% from baseline (renal artery stenosis likely), K+ >5.5 mEq/L
- Expected: Cr may rise 20–30% in first month; plateau by month 2–3

**Peri-op:** Hold 24h pre-op unless cardiology explicitly documents ongoing need; resume post-op if SBP >90 mmHg

## Beta-Blocker Management (HFrEF)
**Guideline-Directed Target Doses (ESC 2023):**
- Bisoprolol: 10 mg daily
- Carvedilol: 25 mg BID
- Metoprolol succinate (XL): 190 mg daily
**Note:** Metoprolol tartrate (immediate-release) is NOT indicated for HFrEF

**Titration:** Double dose every 2 weeks if tolerated; target HR 50–60 bpm at rest
**Peri-op:** ALWAYS continue beta-blockers unless HR <45 or SBP <85 mmHg

## Diuretic Protocols (Volume Management)
**Indication:** Signs of fluid overload (edema, dyspnea, elevated JVP, elevated BNP)
- **Loop diuretics (first-line):** Furosemide 20–40 mg daily, titrate to euvolemia
- **Target:** Symptom relief + euvolemia; avoid over-diuresis (renal dysfunction, hypotension)
- **Monitoring:** Electrolytes (Na, K, Mg, Cl) monthly; renal function and volume status at each visit
- **Caution in CKD Stage 3:** Risk of acute kidney injury with aggressive diuresis; slow uptitration

## Metformin Safety in CKD
**Guidelines (KDIGO 2021, ADA 2024):**
- eGFR >45 mL/min/1.73m²: No dose limit; standard dosing OK
- eGFR 30–44 mL/min/1.73m²: Max 1000 mg/day (half of usual maintenance)
- eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²: CONTRAINDICATED (lactic acidosis risk)
- **Monitoring:** Annual eGFR; assess renal function before non-emergent imaging with contrast

## GLP-1 Agonist Use in CKD/Diabetes
**Indication:** T2DM + HF or CKD (Class I for cardiovascular risk reduction, ADA 2024)
- **Options:** Semaglutide, dulaglutide, liraglutide (GLP-1) or tirzepatide (GLP-1/GIP dual)
- **HFpEF:** Semaglutide reduces HF hospitalizations (Class IIb evidence)
- **CKD:** All reduce albuminuria by 20–40%; slow eGFR decline
- **Initiation:** Slow start (GI side effects); titrate over 4–8 weeks
- **Caution:** Acute pancreatitis risk (rare); counsel on symptoms

## Peri-operative Safety Overrides
**72–48h Pre-op:**
- HOLD: SGLT2 inhibitors (euglycemic DKA risk), ACEi/ARB (hypotension under anesthesia—unless cardiologist documents exception)
- CONTINUE: Beta-blockers (essential), diuretics (if severe volume overload)
- HOLD: GLP-1 agonists 24h pre-op (gastroparesis risk with anesthesia)

**Post-op Restart:**
- Resume SGLT2i, ACEi/ARB, GLP-1, diuretics when oral intake tolerated and serum Cr stable
- Monitor volume status, K+, and renal function daily × 3 days, then at discharge

## Documentation Requirements
- If EF is missing: **Explicitly state:** "HF phenotype unclear; EF not provided."
- If eGFR or HbA1c missing: **Flag as missing data** and request (avoid assumptions).
- **Medication reconciliation:** Confirm current medications; flag any gaps (e.g., "SGLT2i not prescribed—rationale needed").

## Output Preferences  
- **Avoid duplication:** If all 3 specialists recommend SGLT2i, mention ONCE in final synthesis with "Multi-specialist consensus."
- **Clear ownership:** Use format "**Owner:** Cardiology | **Timeline:** Initiate within 48h; titrate over 4 weeks."
- **Risk flagging:** Highlight **hyperkalemia**, **hypotension**, **renal decline**—don't bury in text.
- **Peri-op readiness:** Always include a **"Peri-operative Considerations"** section if procedures are mentioned.
