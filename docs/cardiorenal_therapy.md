# Cardiorenal Therapy Guidelines

Evidence-based medication management for patients with overlapping heart failure, chronic kidney disease, and type 2 diabetes.

## SGLT2 Inhibitors (Cardiorenal)
- In HFrEF and/or CKD with T2DM, SGLT2 inhibitors are generally guideline-supported unless contraindicated.
- Typical starts: empagliflozin 10 mg daily or dapagliflozin 10 mg daily.
- Peri-op: hold 3-4 days pre-op; restart when eating/drinking, stable hemodynamics, and no AKI.
- Do not list hyperkalemia as a typical SGLT2 adverse effect.

## Metformin in CKD (Safety)
- eGFR >= 45: continue standard dosing if tolerated.
- eGFR 30-44: reduce dose (commonly max 1000 mg/day) and monitor renal function closely.
- eGFR < 30: avoid/discontinue due to lactic acidosis risk.
- If contrast/AKI risk exists, reassess temporary hold per institutional protocol.

## RAAS / ARNI in HFrEF + CKD
- Continue/initiate ACEi/ARB/ARNI when indicated and tolerated.
- Monitor creatinine and potassium after initiation or up-titration.
- Reassess if significant creatinine rise, hyperkalemia, or symptomatic hypotension.
- Peri-op: ACEi/ARB commonly held about 24h pre-op depending on anesthesia plan and hemodynamics.

## Beta-Blockers in HFrEF
- Continue guideline-directed beta-blocker therapy in stable HFrEF.
- Peri-op: generally continue to avoid withdrawal, unless a clear contraindication is present.

## MRA (Spironolactone/Eplerenone)
- Consider in eligible HFrEF patients with potassium and renal function permitting.
- Monitor potassium/creatinine at baseline and early after start or dose increase.

## Diuretics and Volume
- Use loop diuretics for congestion symptoms/signs.
- Titrate to euvolemia and avoid over-diuresis, especially in CKD.

## GLP-1 RA in T2DM + CKD/CV Risk
- Consider GLP-1 RA for glycemic/CV benefit when appropriate.
- Use as complementary to cardiorenal strategy based on patient phenotype and tolerance.

## Peri-Operative Medication Quick Rules
- Hold pre-op: SGLT2 inhibitors (3-4 days), metformin on day of surgery (or longer with AKI/contrast risk), ACEi/ARB often 24h pre-op.
- Continue pre-op: beta-blockers, statins (unless contraindicated).
- Restart post-op based on renal function, volume status, hemodynamics, and oral intake.
